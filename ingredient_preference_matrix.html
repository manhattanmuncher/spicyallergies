⚠ SAFETY NOTICE — READ BEFORE USE FOR ALLERGY CLIENTS
This matrix was generated by classifying ingredient NAMES with food knowledge, NOT from
your database tags. The source ingredients table's 'ingredientTypeIds' join column was empty
for ~315 of 324 rows (only an 'alcohol' tag on 9 items existed), so a literal table join
would have matched almost nothing and falsely marked allergens as safe.

Because real allergen status depends on the specific BRAND and RECIPE (e.g. whether a given
Worcestershire uses barley malt, or a bouillon cube contains celery), this file is a
best-effort STARTING POINT. A qualified person MUST verify every allergy cell against the
actual product label before any client with an allergy relies on it. Pay special attention
to every 'X?' and every row where 'Verify label' = Yes.

DATA SOURCES & METHODOLOGY
1) Allergy categories (which preferences are allergies): taken directly from the source
preferences database — every preference flagged metadata.is_allergy = true.
2) Regulatory basis (Denmark/EU): 13 of the 18 columns correspond to STATUTORY allergens
under EU Regulation 1169/2011, Annex II — the list enforced in Denmark by Fødevarestyrelsen:
cereals containing gluten (→ Wheat & Gluten columns), crustaceans + molluscs (→ Shellfish),
fish, eggs, peanuts, soybeans (→ Soy), milk (→ Milk; lactose is the milk sugar), tree nuts,
celery, mustard, sesame, and sulphites. (Lupin, the 14th, has no column here.)
3) NON-statutory columns: Lactose, Fructose, Citrus, Stone fruit and Histamine are intolerances,
NOT listed in Annex II and NOT tracked by any allergen-declaration database. They rely on
general clinical/food knowledge only — treat them as guidance, not regulated allergen data.
4) Ingredient→allergen assignments (every X / X? cell): a best-effort classification grounded in
the Annex II allergen definitions plus general food-composition knowledge. They were NOT
auto-pulled from a product database: automated name-matching against a crowdsourced product
database (Open Food Facts) was tested and rejected because loose matches injected FALSE
allergens (e.g. a generic 'sumac' search returned milk). The full rule set is in the
'Logic & assumptions' tab and build_matrix.py.
5) EMPIRICAL VERIFICATION: to confirm a real product, use the companion tool
'product_allergen_lookup.html' — it queries Open Food Facts live (current, label-derived
data) by barcode for the SPECIFIC product you buy. That is the correct point of empirical
verification. Low FODMAP follows the Monash University criteria cited in your own data.

SCOPE
Columns cover the 18 allergy/intolerance preferences and 16 diet preferences.
The 'Available in <country>' preferences are NOT included — there is no data to infer them.

KEY ASSUMPTIONS APPLIED (see 'Logic & assumptions' tab for the full list)
• Regular soy sauce = wheat + gluten + soy; 'gluten free' soy sauce & tamari = soy only.
• Worcestershire = fish (anchovy) + likely gluten/malt; 'vegan' Worcestershire = neither.
• Asafetida / Hing powder is cut with WHEAT flour → wheat + gluten.
• Bouillon / stock / broth → celery + gluten + meat all flagged 'X?' (verify).
• Commercial wasabi contains mustard. Almond extract = tree nut.
• Lemongrass and lemon balm are NOT citrus despite the name.

ALLERGENS
Fish: fish sauce, fish broth, Worcestershire (anchovy). 'Fish spice' flagged X? (verify).
Shellfish: oyster sauce, cuttlefish ink. Fish sauce X? (may contain shrimp).
Milk/Lactose: cheddar, sour cream, ranch (buttermilk), cream/butter items, dairy flavor powders.
Egg: ranch/mayo/aioli/hollandaise flagged X?. Aquafaba is an egg SUBSTITUTE (not flagged).
Peanut: explicit peanut/satay = X; goreng/biryani/curry paste = X?.
Tree nut: almond/hazelnut/walnut/etc. Almond also X? for stone fruit cross-reactivity.
Soy: soy sauce, tamari, miso, hoisin, teriyaki, liquid aminos = X. Sambal/sriracha/oyster/
browning/goreng/Worcestershire = X?. Coconut aminos = soy-free (not flagged).
Sesame: everything bagel seasoning, tahini, za'atar = X. Hoisin/goreng = X?.
Mustard: all mustards + commercial wasabi = X. BBQ/ranch/pickle/curry powder/Worcestershire = X?.
Celery: celery powder, Old Bay, Vegeta = X. Bouillon/stock/broth + savory blends = X?.
Wheat: regular soy sauce, asafetida (wheat-cut), beer(X?), hoisin, teriyaki = X. Stock = X?.
Gluten: regular soy sauce, beer/malt, asafetida, hoisin, teriyaki = X. Worcestershire/miso/stock = X?.
Fructose: table sugar, balsamic, honey, maple, molasses = X. Fruit juices & sweet sauces = X?.
Sugar-free / erythritol / stevia / monkfruit / allulose = NOT flagged.
Sulfite: wine, beer, sherry, balsamic, wine vinegar = X. Dried chiles, other vinegars, pickled = X?.
Citrus: lemon/lime/orange (juice, zest, slices), lemon pepper, Tajín, lime leaves = X.
Lemongrass and lemon balm are explicitly NOT citrus.
Histamine (broad): fermented/aged/vinegar/alcohol/pickled = X; citrus/cocoa/chili/smoked = X?.

DIETS
Vegan: all fish/shellfish/dairy/egg/gelatin + meat stock(X?).
Vegetarian: fish/shellfish/meat/gelatin (dairy & egg allowed).
Pescatarian: mammal/poultry meat + gelatin; fish & shellfish allowed.
No red meat / No pork: gelatin (X?) and meat stock (X?); no whole pork/red-meat items in dataset.
Halal: alcoholic beverages + wine vinegars (per DB tag) = X; extracts/gelatin/stock = X?.
Kosher: shellfish (oyster, cuttlefish) = X; gelatin/stock = X?.
Paleo: grains/legumes (soy, beer, corn starch), refined sugar, dairy = X; sweeteners/additives = X?.
Whole foods: any branded or processed product (sauces, blends, sweeteners, supplements, colorings...).
Vegan whole food: union of Vegan-excluded and Whole-foods-excluded.
Supplement free: creatine, pre-workout, L-carnitine, MAP, maca, spirulina, protein/flavor powders.
Diabetes friendly: added-sugar / refined-carb items (sugar-free versions NOT flagged).
PCOS friendly: same as diabetes PLUS artificial sweeteners (sucralose/aspartame) = X?.
Pregnancy friendly: alcohol, supplements, and herbals (licorice=X; raspberry leaf/nettle/fenugreek/sage=X?).
Family friendly: protein supplements/shakes.
Low FODMAP / IBS: garlic & onion family = X; lactose/wheat items, high-FODMAP fruit, blends = X?.
