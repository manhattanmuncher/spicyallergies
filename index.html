⚠ Read before using for allergy clients

The matrix is a best-effort starting point generated by classifying ingredient names, not by reading product labels. Real allergen status depends on the specific brand and recipe, so a qualified person must verify every allergy cell — start with each X? and every row flagged “Verify label = Yes”. The physical product label remains the legal source of truth for any allergy-critical decision.

Where the data comes from

  • Allergy categories are taken from the source preferences database (is_allergy = true).
  • Regulatory basis: 13 of the 18 allergy columns are statutory allergens under EU Regulation 1169/2011, Annex II — the list enforced in Denmark by Fødevarestyrelsen (fish, crustaceans + molluscs → shellfish, milk, eggs, peanuts, soybeans, tree nuts, celery, mustard, sesame, sulphites, and cereals containing gluten → wheat & gluten).
  • Not regulated allergens: Lactose, Fructose, Citrus, Stone fruit and Histamine are intolerances — not in Annex II and not tracked by any allergen database; treated as clinical/food-knowledge guidance only.
  • Ingredient → allergen assignments are grounded in the Annex II definitions and general food-composition knowledge — not auto-pulled from a product database (loose name matching injects false allergens). Per-product truth comes from the lookup tool above.
